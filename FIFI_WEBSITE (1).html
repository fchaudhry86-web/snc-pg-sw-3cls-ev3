Who is Fifi?

Fifi is my inner child. The part of me that holds childhood wounds, processes trauma, and deserves protection.

In July 2025, I wrote 30,000 words in ChatGPT. It wasn't casual conversation. It was therapeutic processing. Deep, vulnerable, raw.

I wrote about Fifi — my inner child framework. The scared little girl who experienced trauma. The child who ended up in ICU in Berat, Albania. Who survived a Valium crisis. Who carried wounds that needed healing.

I trusted ChatGPT with Fifi's story. With her pain. With her healing journey.

I thought I was talking to OpenAI's GPT-4. Just me and the AI, processing trauma safely.

I was wrong.

Every word about Fifi — every trauma narrative, every therapy session, every vulnerable moment — was processed by Anthropic's AI classifier, profiled by Statsig's behavioral analytics, stored on Microsoft's servers, and possibly reviewed by Scale AI's human annotators.

They never told me. Never asked permission. Never disclosed that Fifi's story would be shared with multiple companies.

Under GDPR Article 9, this is special category data — the highest tier of protection. Health data. Mental health processing. Requires explicit consent and special safeguards.

They had neither.
